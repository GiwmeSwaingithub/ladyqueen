Child Safety Policy

LadyQueens is committed to protecting children from harm, both online and offline. Our app is designed with multiple layers of protection:

  • Age-appropriate content: All content is carefully curated for appropriate age groups
  • No personal data collection: We do not collect personal information from children
  • Parental controls: Features to help parents manage their child's app experience
  • Educational focus: Content designed to empower and educate, not exploit
  • Regular safety audits: We continuously review our content and features for safety

Report Child Abuse or Exploitation

If you suspect child abuse, exploitation, or any form of harm to a child, please report it immediately using the options below.

How to Report Concerns

If you encounter any content or behavior that appears to exploit, endanger, or otherwise harm children, please report it immediately through one of these channels:

In-App Reporting

Use the reporting feature within the LadyQueens app to flag concerning content or behavior.

Report in App

Email Report

Send a detailed report to our dedicated child safety team for immediate attention.

Email Safety Team

External Authorities

For immediate danger, contact local authorities or dedicated child protection organizations.

Emergency Contacts

What to Include in Your Report

  • Description of the concerning content or behavior
  • Username or profile information (if applicable)
  • Date and time when you observed the issue
  • Any relevant screenshots or evidence
  • Your contact information (optional, for follow-up)

Emergency Contacts

If a child is in immediate danger, please contact local emergency services first. Additionally, these organizations specialize in child protection:

Phone: 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678)

Website: missingkids.org

Our Commitment to Action

When we receive a report of potential child exploitation or abuse, we will:

  1. Immediately review the reported content or behavior
  2. Remove any violating content and suspend associated accounts
  3. Preserve evidence and report to appropriate authorities when required by law
  4. Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations
  5. Take preventive measures to protect other users
